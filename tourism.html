History

Pune is one of the oldest cities in India with a rich historical background. It was the center of the Maratha Empire and has many historical landmarks.

Attraction

Pune is a city of many attractions, from historical landmarks to natural beauty. Some are below:

Shaniwar Wada

Shaniwar Wada is a historical fortification in the city of Pune, India. Built in 1732,[1] it was the great seat of the Peshwas of the Maratha Empire until 1818.

Aga Khan Palace

The Aga Khan Palace was built by Sultan Muhammed Shah Aga Khan III in the city of Pune, India.

Parvati Hills

Parvati Hill is a hillock in Pune, India. The hillock rises to 2,100 feet (640 m) above sea level.

Culture

Pune exemplifies an indigenous Marathi culture and ethos, in which education, arts and crafts, and theatres are given due prominence.

Food

When in Pune, be sure to not miss out on delicacies like Misal Pav, Pithla Bhakri, Bhakarwadi, Mastani, Dabeli, Pav Bhaji, Poha, Vada Pav and baked goodies from the old school bakeries.
